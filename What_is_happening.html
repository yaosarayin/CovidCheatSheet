What exactly is COVID-19?
(i.e. the Coronavirus)

COVID-19 is a virus, similar to the flu, but spreads way faster

A virus is a tiny piece of DNA that we cannot see. It can attack your body’s cells and replicate itself.

What is a virus?

We have an immune system that attacks viruses. Our nose running, our temperature rising, are all your immune system doing its job!

Why do we get “sick”?

It spreads from person to person by being in contact with your eyes, nose or mouth. Sharing food, breathing, shaking hands, even touching the same doorknob and then touching your face can spread the virus.

How does it spread?
Fever, Cough, Shortness of Breath

How did the world respond?

We tested over 320,000 cases early on in only Guangdong, and much more across the rest of the country.

Wuhan, the epicenter of the outbreak, went into complete lockdown where no one was allowed to leave the region by any means.

We moved 50% of all medical care online to make room for hospitals to de-densify hospitals and to concentrate care.

If someone was tested positive, they would be isolated in the hospital. They limited infection to hospitalization down to two days which was critical to limit the spread.

All testing was free!
Over 40,000 medical professionals were sent to Wuhan to help the epicenter.
15 million people in Wuhan ordered food online and had it delivered.
Schools across the country closed, but classes were still held online.

Most public schools have been closed in all states.
Most private college campuses have been closed as well; all college classes have moved online.
Restaurants have been closed, but takeout can still be ordered.
Sporting events, including March Madness and Olympics, were either cancelled or postponed.
Concert tours have been called off or postponed.
Most states have called for a “shelter-in-place”

Convention Centers are being turned into makeshift medical centers.
Hospital Ships are housing patients offshore
Some states are screening people with out-of-state license plates.

What Happened to the Economy?

Unfortunately, because people can’t go to work as well, the country’s productivity has gone down significantly. This has led to significant drops in the stock market. This means that people are losing money, on top of this global pandemic.

This drop in the economy has been the worst drop in the stock market following the Great Depression in the 1930s.

And along with this, people are also losing jobs as companies can’t afford to pay their workers any longer. Unemployment claims have increased by 3 million just last week.

In order to help people losing money and jobs, the US has also given over $200 billion dollars worth of housing debt back to the people so they can use that money for other necessities.

If your family has been affected, be aware that the adults in the household might be stressed. Help out as much as you can around the house, take responsibilities, we will get through this together.

The United States Federal Reserve in response to the growing economic fallout of the COV-19 epidemic slashed federal interest rates by 1% to a range of 0 to 0.25% interest rates to help inject money into the economy and promote lending.

The Fed also announced plans to purchase $500 billion dollars worth of Treasury Securities and $200 billion dollars worth of mortgage-backed debt to help inject money directly into the economy.

The Dow Jones Industrial has plunged over 25% in value over the last month following record highs at the end of February. This downturn was worse than the stock market drop following the great depression and has erased most of the gains since Donald Trump has become president.
