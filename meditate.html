Meditate

Sit or lie down comfortably in a quiet room. Ideally, the room is not too bright. Then try each route for about 5 minutes first. Go for longer if it feels good!

Concentrate on one spot of your body with your eyes and concentrate on that spot.

If you feel your concentration slipping away, reconcentrate on that spot.

One trick is to focus on your breathing, concentrate on your breaths in and out.

Close your eyes and observe the thoughts that pop into your head.

However, try not to dwell on your thoughts! Label them “thinking”

As you feel your mind thinking about a certain thought, refocus and let the thought go.

Why Meditate?

  • Meditation is important to relieve stress and anxiety in anyone’s day. It allows people to identify their priorities and deal with difficult situations more easily.
  • Meditation can decrease physical tension and strengthen your immune system.
  • Meditation for young children can be helpful and can promote happiness. Young children who practice mindfulness are shown to be less judgmental and they can develop healthy mental habits early on.
  • If you are worried about the impact of COVID-19, practicing mindfulness is a great way to relax and deal with these difficult stresses more confidently.
